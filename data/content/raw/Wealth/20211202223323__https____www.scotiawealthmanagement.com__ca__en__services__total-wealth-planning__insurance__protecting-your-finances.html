Protect your family and finances with insurance

Protect your family and your finances from the impact of certain unexpected events

Insurance is best known for protecting you and your family from the risk of financial loss and safeguarding your wealth in a number of ways. A Total Wealth Plan includes an assessment of your financial risk in the event of a serious illness, disability or untimely death. Your ability to earn an income is one of the most significant assets you have; having an insurance plan in place to protect this valuable asset will alleviate financial stress during a period of personal crisis.  

There are a number of solutions and strategies to help you manage risk and protect you against future financial loss:

  • Critical illness insurance

    Dealing with an illness can be very expensive. The financial impact from medical and recovery costs not covered through government plans can result in a significant financial setback. Critical Illness (CI) insurance is a type of coverage that can protect your financial health. A CI policy will cover a range of illnesses outlined in the contract (e.g. cancer, heart attack); if the insured is diagnosed with one of those covered conditions then after a prescribed waiting period the policy will pay a lump sum, tax-free benefit. Having CI insurance ensures that if you do fall ill, your medical concerns will not be compounded by financial ones.

  • Disability insurance 

    Disability insurance helps protect one of your most valuable assets, your ability to earn an income. The risk of becoming disabled for a significant period of time before age 65 is actually higher than the risk of dying before that age. A disability insurance (DI) policy manages that risk by providing a monthly income benefit for a predetermined duration – usually to age 65 – if you become disabled (based on the policy’s definition of disability). Policies usually include a waiting period before benefit payments begin and there are various riders that can be selected to enhance coverage at an additional cost, such as ensuring benefits keep pace with inflation.

    Be aware of group coverage limitations.  Although many employers provide group DI plans that provide tremendous value, there are some limitations to a group disability policy you should be aware of. For instance, plans are owned by the employer and may be cancelled or changed at any time. Secondly, coverage is often terminated if you leave the company. If you already have group disability coverage, you may want to consider supplementing that plan with your own individual disability insurance policy. That way, you can mitigate these limitations, benefit from the protection of complementary solutions, and maximize your income sources when it is needed the most.

  • Term insurance 

    Term insurance is a cost-effective way to cover off temporary liabilities. Often looked at as renting protection; it is generally used to replace future income or to cover specific financial obligations in the event of the insured’s death, such as paying off a mortgage and other debts, or funding post-secondary education. A specific amount of insurance coverage would be purchased for a specific period of time (the “term”), such as 10 years. If the insured passes away while the coverage is in place, then a tax-free death benefit is payable directly to the named beneficiaries of the insurance policy.

  • Managing business risks with insurance

    Business owners have a significant opportunity to build considerable wealth. However, this opportunity also comes with significant responsibility as it relates to the risk involved with running a business. 

    The death or illness of a key employee or shareholder could have a devastating impact on the business, especially if the individual that passes away is a key driver of the business or the death triggers a buy-sell provision that the remaining shareholders cannot absorb. There are insurance strategies that can assist owners with mitigating these risks and others, allowing you, the business owner to focus on what you do best running your business.

    There are a number of solutions and strategies to help you manage risk and protect your business against future financial loss:

    Key person insurance 

    The purpose of key person insurance would be to provide the company with the financial ability to survive losing a person who is crucial to the operation of the business. The company would purchase key person protection on that individual in the way of a life insurance policy If the insured key person died, the policy would provide a cash payment that can be used to ease the financial well-being of the company. This would allow the business to survive such a loss and provide the time needed to recruit a replacement without having additional financial stresses. 

    Business loan insurance 

    Business loan insurance can provide businesses with financial relief to ensure loan obligations are met in the event of an owner’s death. The business would purchase a life insurance policy on the life of the business owner(s); should the business owner(s) pass away, the policy would pay out the death benefit. The proceeds are then used to create immediate capital for the business to repay the business debt. With having business loan insurance, companies can minimize potential cash flow problems and keep other business assets intact, allowing the business to continue thriving. Some lenders may even require a life insurance policy be collaterally assigned to secure the loan in the first place.

    Buy-sell insurance 

    With businesses consisting of one or more business partners, a shareholder/partnership agreement likely requires that there is a buy-sell provision in place, which outlines what should be done in the event of the death, disability or retirement of one of the shareholders. The agreement is meant to simplify the eventual buy-out by establishing valuation methods, terms of sale and other important components. There must be funding in place that ensures the terms set out in the agreement are met when the other shareholders are called on to purchase the business interest.  Life Insurance and/or disability insurance policies can be put in place on each owner. The policy would provide a cash payment that can be used by the other owner(s) to buy out the interest of the disabled or deceased owner’s share of the business. Providing this funding through insurance allows for an effective way of providing the necessary cash required at the time it is needed most.
