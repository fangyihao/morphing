Optimize your wealth with insurance 

Optimize your wealth by minimizing, deferring or offsetting your tax liability

We can help you optimize your wealth by minimizing, deferring, or offsetting your tax liability. Life insurance can provide tax-sheltered growth and tax-free payouts, essentially allowing you to grow part of your wealth tax-free for the benefit of the next generation.  In many cases, the positive impact insurance can have on a portfolio inspires our clients to consider it as an alternative to a traditional conservative investment.  If you have investments inside a corporation, those benefits can be even more compelling – reducing fully taxable investment income and creating tax-free shareholder dividends to distribute the death benefit proceeds. There is incredible value here.

  • Benefits of having an Estate Plan 

    While estate planning is a broad topic that includes an assortment of considerations, one of the key areas of emphasis is tax minimization. Taxes have a significant impact on the value of the estate that is ultimately distributed. Preserving your wealth from the effects of this taxation is one of the main ways that life insurance creates real value for investors. The impact is even more stark inside of a corporation, but it works with personal investments as well. Either way, incorporating life insurance into their estate plan allows investors to integrate more tax-efficient investment options into their asset mix and substantially increase the amount of wealth they distribute.  Estate planning is not just about tax minimization but also about ensuring your wealth is transferred how and where you wish it to.

  • Permanent insurance

    Permanent insurance provides you guaranteed lifetime protection from the financial impact your death could cause. As long as the required premiums are paid, you will have insurance coverage from the date the policy is issued to the day you pass away. Typically, permanent insurance is used to address estate planning needs, such as funding taxes owing as a result of death or creating capital that a business might need to facilitate ownership transfers. 

    There are a number of permanent insurance strategies we can investigate to help you build your wealth and protect it from excessive taxation.

    Pre-funding future tax liability strategy 

    Most investors will build retirement wealth using RRSPs, which will transfer to RRIFs; at death any remaining balance becomes fully taxable. Similarly, any accrued gains on assets can result in significant capital gains taxes – the family cottage is a perfect example. While tax liabilities can be “rolled” to a surviving spouse, upon that second death the taxes are payable, which can deplete the estate or, worse, force a cherished asset to be sold. Permanent life insurance is often the perfect solution for this situation as it provides the necessary funds at exactly the time they are needed to address this future liability.

    Asset Transfer 

    The Asset Transfer strategy involves shifting a portion of your assets from fully taxable investments into a tax-exempt life insurance policy.  An insurance policy would be purchased, and the policy could be structured on either a single or joint life basis. Premiums for the policy could either be funded by reallocating assets from a non-registered investment portfolio or by using excess cash flow not needed for annual living expenses. At the death of the insured(s), the policy would pay out a tax-free death benefit directly to the named beneficiaries. The asset transfer strategy will provide a way to diversify your current asset mix in a tax-efficient way, significantly increasing the net estate value available for the next generation.

    Insured Annuity 

    One of the biggest risks you’ll face during this stage of your life is longevity risk - the risk you’ll outlive your income. A life annuity is one of the few ways that you can guarantee lifetime income, with potential tax benefits and possible protection from inflation. The Insured Annuity strategy has two parts: , a one-time deposit  to acquire a life annuity which creates a lifetime of regular payments that are a combination of both interest and principal. Only the interest portion is taxable, which makes the payments tax-efficient, and since each payment includes your original capital, the after tax cash flow is considerably higher than other investments. But that also means the capital is gone when you die (unless structured with guarantees). To address that, the First part of this strategy requires the  purchase of a permanent life insurance policy equal to the amount of assets being allocated toward the strategy, using a  part of the after-tax cash flow the annuity produces to pay the premiums, which replenishes the original capital investment at death. The net result is similar to a fixed income investment but typically with a higher after-tax income.

  • Optimizing your business 

    In addition to helping business owners manage risks, insurance strategies can also be used for minimizing taxes, unlocking trapped corporate surpluses, enhancing your overall financial position and creating a larger estate to pass to your heirs. 

    While many Canadian corporations invest their surplus cash or retained profits in taxable investments, any income or growth from these non-registered investments, such as GICs, stocks, bonds, real estate and daily interest, will be taxable to some extent– either as it is earned or upon the distribution of the asset. Even upon death, all assets are deemed to be sold at their fair market value for tax purposes; these assets can not only be exposed to tax at the corporate level but also at a personal level. When the dividends are paid out of the corporation, this can result in a significant tax liability. Many investors are simply looking to create a diversified pool of assets with stable returns that will attract the least amount of tax during their lifetime and transition that wealth to the next generation in a tax-efficient way.

    Working with your advisor, here are a number of solutions and strategies we can investigate to help you optimize your wealth and protect your business from excessive taxation:

    Corporate Asset Transfer  

    A permanent life insurance policy would be purchased by the corporation who would own the policy.  The policy could be structured on either a single or joint life basis. Premiums for the policy could either be funded by reallocating passive investment assets held inside the corporation or by using excess cash flow not required by the corporation. At the death of the insured(s), the life insurance death benefit would pay out a tax-free death benefit directly to the corporation. The corporation would receive credit to its capital dividend account (CDA) for the amount of the insurance proceeds minus the insurance policy’s adjust cost basis (ACB). Capital dividends can then be paid out tax-free to the owner’s estate.

    The corporate asset transfer strategy provides a way to diversify the corporation’s current asset mix in a tax-efficient way, significantly increasing the net estate value.

    Corporate Immediate Financing Arrangement 

    Owners of successful private corporations are well aware of the importance of cash flow and how they allocate corporate capital so that business ventures are funded and investment opportunities are not missed. The Corporate Immediate Financing Arrangement is when a permanent life insurance policy is purchased by the corporation, which would own the policy, pay the premiums, and be the named beneficiary of the policy. The permanent life insurance policy is then collaterally assigned for loan advances, which are then invested, either in an investment account, or real estate, or back into the business.  Upon the death of the insured, the corporation is entitled to the life insurance proceeds tax-free, minus the amount owed to the collateral assignee. This also results in a credit to the corporation’s capital dividend account (CDA) for the amount of life insurance proceeds (prior to repaying the loan) minus the insurance policy’s adjusted cost basis (ACB). Capital dividends may then be paid tax-free to the owner’s estate. 

    Corporate Insured Annuity 

    The Corporate Insured Annuity strategy is an insurance-based financial planning strategy designed to enhance the income of business owners while still maintaining a legacy for their heirs.  The insured annuity is made up of two separate components: the corporation will purchase a life insurance policy with the business owner as the life insured and the corporation named as the beneficiary.  Once approved, part of the corporation’s conservative investment portfolio is liquidated, and the funds are used to purchase a life annuity. The cash flow generated by the life annuity is used to pay the life insurance premiums on the policy and the taxes payable on the annuity. The remaining after-tax income is paid out to the business owner as a dividend. 

    Upon the insured’s death, the life insurance proceeds are paid to the corporation and generate a credit to its Capital Dividend Account equal to the death benefit paid, minus the adjusted cost basis.  These proceeds can then be paid out tax-free to the business owner’s estate, through a tax-free capital dividend and distributed to the business owner’s heirs or charity as stipulated in the Will.
